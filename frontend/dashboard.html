Tax Regimes: Old vs New

Choose the regime that maximizes your savings. You can switch regimes each financial year.

Old Tax Regime

With Deductions & Exemptions

Allows all deductions (80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, etc.)
Standard Deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried individuals
HRA exemption, LTA, interest on home loan deductions
Deductions for donations (80G), education loan (80E)
Higher compliance & documentation required
Best for: Those with high investments & multiple deductions

New Tax Regime

Simplified with Lower Rates

Simplified tax structure with fewer slabs
Lower tax rates for most income brackets
Standard Deduction: ₹75,000 for salaried individuals
Most exemptions and deductions not allowed
No HRA, LTA, 80C, 80D, 80E deductions
Best for: Salaried with minimal investments & deductions

Income Tax Return Forms

Select the appropriate ITR form based on your income sources and category

ITR-1 (Sahaj)

For resident individuals with salary/pension income, one house property, and income from other sources (interest, dividends up to ₹5,000).

Salary up to ₹50 lakhs | No business income

ITR-2

For individuals & HUFs not having income from business or profession. Includes capital gains, foreign assets/income, and multiple house properties.

No business income | Capital gains applicable

ITR-3

For individuals & HUFs having income from business or profession. Includes partnership firms, professionals, and presumptive taxation.

Business/profession income | Partnership firms

ITR-4 (Sugam)

For individuals, HUFs and firms with presumptive income from business & profession under sections 44AD, 44ADA, and 44AE.

Presumptive taxation | Turnover up to ₹2 crores

ITR Filing Process

Step-by-step guide to file your Income Tax Return accurately and efficiently

1

Gather Documents

Collect Form 16 (from employer), bank statements, investment proofs (80C, 80D), rent receipts, home loan interest certificate, and previous year's ITR copy.

2

Choose Correct ITR Form

Select appropriate ITR form based on your income sources. Most salaried individuals use ITR-1 (Sahaj). Business owners and professionals need ITR-3 or ITR-4.

3

Compute Total Income

Calculate income under all five heads: Salary, House Property, Business/Profession, Capital Gains, and Other Sources. Consider deductions and exemptions.

4

Claim Deductions

Apply eligible deductions under Chapter VI-A (80C, 80D, 80E, etc.), HRA exemption, standard deduction, and other applicable tax-saving provisions.

5

Calculate Tax Liability

Compute final tax payable after considering TDS, advance tax paid, and tax deducted at source. Determine if you need to pay additional tax or get a refund.

6

E-Verify & Submit

E-verify using Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or send signed ITR-V to CPC Bengaluru within 120 days. Keep acknowledgment for future reference.

Popular Tax Deductions

Maximize your tax savings with these commonly used deductions

Section 80C

₹1.5L

Investments in PPF, ELSS, LIC, NSC, 5-year FD, Home Loan Principal, Sukanya Samriddhi, EPF, Tuition fees.

Section 80D

₹1L

Health insurance premium for self (₹25,000), parents (₹25,000), senior citizens (₹50,000 each). Preventive health check-ups (₹5,000).

HRA Exemption

Actual

Minimum of: 1) Actual HRA received, 2) 50% of salary (metro) or 40% (non-metro), 3) Rent paid minus 10% of salary. Rent receipts required.

Section 24(b)

₹2L

Interest on home loan for self-occupied property (max ₹2 lakhs). No limit for let-out property. Loan must be for purchase/construction.

NPS 80CCD(1B)

₹50K

Additional ₹50,000 deduction for NPS investment over and above ₹1.5 lakhs of Section 80C. Available to all individuals.

Section 80E

No Limit

Interest on education loan for higher education (self, spouse, children). Available for 8 years from commencement of repayment. No upper limit.

Standard Deduction

₹75K

New regime: ₹75,000 | Old regime: ₹50,000. Available to all salaried individuals and pensioners. No proof required.

Section 80G

50-100%

Donations to approved charities and institutions. 50% or 100% of donation amount deductible depending on the institution.
